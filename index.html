Insight #1 Top Skills

The best skills tend to be system, social, and resource management skills. They require the wide perspective on self, others, and tasks. These skills are difficult to master. They are well rewarded by high income and security.

Insight #2 Bottom Skills

The worst skills tend to be technical skills. They require the narrow perspectives on specific tools and tasks. Even though they are easy to master, these skills tend to be associated with low-paid and insecure jobs.

Insight #3 Learnability VS Income

There is a strong negative correlation between learnability and income. Given the same learnability, system, resource management, and complex solving skills are associated with higher income.

Insight #4 Income VS Security

There is a strong positive correlation between income and security. Given the same income, social skills are associated with higher security.

Insight #5 Income VS Satisfaction

The satisfaction is slightly higher when the income is higher. Given the same income, social skills are associated with higher satisfaction.
